About

What is Amity?

Amity is a shared loyalty identity. Instead of each shopper having their own Clubcard-style account, multiple people use the same Amity ID, blending their purchases into a single, collective data trail.

Shared customer

Amity acts as one supermarket customer made from many people’s behaviour. The system cannot easily separate who is who.

System visibility

Watching how Amity is rewarded, targeted and profiled reveals how loyalty schemes quietly shape everyday shopping.

Collective agency

By pooling identity and rewards, the collective gains power over where points go and how offers circulate – instead of that logic being set by algorithms alone.

System

How Amity interferes with loyalty cards

Loyalty systems rely on consistent, individual data: scan, spend, repeat. Amity introduces inconsistency and shared use.

01 · Sign-up

Amity is registered as a customer on multiple supermarket schemes. On paper, she looks like a single enthusiastic shopper.

02 · Shared scanning

Copies of the Amity QR code circulate. Different people, in different places and at different times, shop as Amity.

03 · Data noise

The resulting behaviour pattern is contradictory and difficult to profile: overlapping lifestyles, locations and routines.

Collective

The Amity Collective

The Amity Collective is the group of people who choose to share this identity and experiment with alternative ways of organising loyalty, reward and data.

Principles
  • Anyone can temporarily “be” Amity.
  • No single person owns the account.
  • Loyalty is redirected from brands to the collective.
Experiments
  • Shared supermarket loyalty cards and QR codes.
  • Ghost receipts and daily data trails.
  • Random / committee-based reward distribution.
  • Fruit stickers and posters that hijack store aesthetics.
Ambitions
  • Expose how loyalty systems manage behaviour.
  • Disrupt clean profiling with collective identities.
  • Find fairer, shared forms of reward.
Join

How to (theoretically) join Amity

Joining Amity means opting into a shared loyalty identity and helping decide what happens to its points, offers and data.

  • Receive an Amity card or QR code instead of a personal one.
  • Scan as Amity when you shop at participating supermarkets.
  • Let your purchases merge into the shared Amity profile.
  • Take part in collective decisions about rewards and experiments.

This work does not encourage breaking store terms; it explores alternative futures for loyalty, identity and data.
